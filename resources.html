Community Organizations

1. The Queer Muslim Project

View this profile on Instagram

The Queer Muslim Project (@thequeermuslimproject) • Instagram photos and videos

The Queer Muslim Project (TQMP), an Indian non-profit organization that advocates for the rights and well-being of queer Muslims around the world. Their content is geared towards raising awareness about the unique challenges and needs of queer Muslims, and to provide a platform for queer Muslims to connect and express themselves. The account features a range of media, including photos, videos, and written content, and art.

2. Muslims for Progressive Values

https://www.mpvusa.org/communities

Muslims for Progressive Values (MPV) is an international grassroots organization that advocates for a values-based understanding of Islam that is centered on social justice, human rights, and inclusivity. MPV is a faith-based organization that promotes a vision of Islam that is open, egalitarian, and progressive, and seeks to challenge the conservative and patriarchal interpretations of Islam that are prevalent in many Muslim-majority countries and communities. The organization works to create safe spaces for marginalized Muslim communities, and to promote interfaith dialogue and understanding between Muslims and people of other faiths. MPV's work includes organizing events and programs, offering education and training, and engaging in advocacy and activism on a range of social and political issues.

Literature

1. We Have Always Been Here by Sammra Habib

"We Have Always Been Here" is a memoir by Samra Habib that tells the story of their journey from growing up as part of a threatened minority in Pakistan, to becoming a refugee in Canada and escaping an arranged marriage at sixteen. The memoir also explores their experience of being queer and feeling like an outsider, and their journey to embrace their true self. The book offers a message of hope, faith, and love, and serves as a rallying cry for anyone who has ever felt alone.

2. The Henna Wars by Adiba Jaigirdar

"The Henna Wars" is a young adult novel by Adiba Jaigirdar that tells the story of Nishat, a Bangladeshi teenager growing up in Ireland, who falls in love with a childhood friend, Flávia. However, their relationship is complicated by the fact that Flávia starts a henna business for their school's entrepreneurial competition, which Nishat feels is appropriating her culture. As the two navigate their feelings for each other and the tensions caused by the henna business, they confront issues of identity, cultural appropriation, and homophobia. Ultimately, the book is a story of love, self-discovery, and fighting for what is right.

3. If They Come For Us by Fatimah Asghar

"If They Come For Us" is a poetry collection by Fatimah Asghar that explores themes of identity, home, loss, and the experiences of being a Muslim in America. The poems are largely autobiographical and draw on the author's experiences growing up as a Pakistani Muslim immigrant in America, as well as her travels to Pakistan. The collection also delves into the historical context of the Partition of India and Pakistan, and how it continues to shape the lives of people in South Asia and in the diaspora. Overall, "If They Come For Us" is a powerful exploration of the complexities of identity and the ways in which personal and historical traumas can shape a person's understanding of themselves and their place in the world.
